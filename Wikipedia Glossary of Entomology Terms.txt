<dt class=""glossary"" id=""capitate"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary"">capitate</dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">Mainly referring to antennae, but occasionally to other anatomical features such as palps: having a clubbed shape with a relatively long, slender stem, but with an abruptly bulkier, thicker, possibly globular <a href=""/wiki/Anatomical_terms_of_location"" title=""Anatomical terms of location"">distal</a> head, the <i>capitulum</i>. The term <i>capitate</i> is not strictly distinguished from <i>clavate</i>, but where a distinction is desired, it is that the club of a capitate antenna is abruptly distinct from the shaft, and the head tends to be short and more or less globular. The club of a clavate antenna generally is a more or less tapered thickening, sometimes hardly distinct from the shaft.</dd>

<dt class=""glossary"" id=""capitulum"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary"">capitulum</dfn></dt>

<dt class=""glossary"" id=""repugnatorial"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/repugnatorial"" class=""extiw"" title=""wiktionary:repugnatorial"">repugnatorial</a></dfn></dt><dd class=""glossary"">(generally in combination as in: ""<b>repugnatorial glands</b>""): defensive, or ""fighting back"", in particular as applied to an <b>ozadene</b>, a gland that can release irritant, poisonous, alarming or distasteful fluids or gases when an organism is under threat. Examples of repugnatorial glands include the <a href=""/wiki/Osmeterium"" title=""Osmeterium"">osmeterium</a> of <a href=""/wiki/Larva"" title=""Larva"">larvae</a> of the <a href=""/wiki/Swallowtail_butterfly"" title=""Swallowtail butterfly"">Papilionidae</a>, the stink glands of most <a href=""/wiki/Heteroptera"" title=""Heteroptera"">Heteroptera</a>, the <a href=""/wiki/Ozadene"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Ozadene"">ozadenes</a> of <a href=""/wiki/Opiliones"" title=""Opiliones"">Opiliones</a>, the odoriferous glands of <a href=""/wiki/Diplopoda"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Diplopoda"">Diplopoda</a>, and others. Some insects, such as larvae of many species of the <a href=""/wiki/Chrysomelidae"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Chrysomelidae"">Chrysomelidae</a>, use their excreta in repugnatorial roles, for example covering themselves with their own frass.</dd>
