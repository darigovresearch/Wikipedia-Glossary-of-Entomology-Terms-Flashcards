"capitate (Wikipedia Glossary of Entomology Terms)"|"
Mainly referring to antennae, but occasionally to other anatomical features such as palps: having a clubbed shape with a relatively long, slender stem, but with an abruptly bulkier, thicker, possibly globular <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatomical_terms_of_location"" title=""Anatomical terms of location"">distal</a> head, the <i>capitulum</i>. The term <i>capitate</i> is not strictly distinguished from <i>clavate</i>, but where a distinction is desired, it is that the club of a capitate antenna is abruptly distinct from the shaft, and the head tends to be short and more or less globular. The club of a clavate antenna generally is a more or less tapered thickening, sometimes hardly distinct from the shaft.
